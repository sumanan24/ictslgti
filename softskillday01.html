Why Soft Skills Are Important

Soft skills support how we think, communicate, and act with others — they shape our success in any environment.

  • They help us work well with others in class, at home, and at work.
  • They support clear communication – sharing ideas and listening actively.
  • They build our ability to solve problems calmly and creatively.
  • They increase self-confidence and help us handle challenges better.

What Are Soft Skills?

Soft skills are personal skills that help people interact and work well with others in any situation.

  • They are linked to how you think, feel, and behave.
  • They show in how you communicate, cooperate, and respond to challenges.
  • They can be practised and improved over time.
Simple idea
Soft skills are about how you work with people, not just what you know.
The human side of work
Attitude, behaviour, and people skills.

Examples of Soft Skills

These skills help you succeed in group work, projects, internships, and jobs.

  • Communication – speaking clearly, listening well.
  • Teamwork – cooperating and supporting others.
  • Leadership – guiding and motivating people.
  • Problem Solving – finding solutions calmly.
  • Time Management – planning and meeting deadlines.
Everyday soft skills
Used in class projects and real workplaces.

Soft vs Technical Skills

What Are Technical Skills?

Technical skills are job-specific or subject-specific skills that can often be measured and tested.

  • Programming – writing code in languages like C, Java, Python.
  • Machine operation – using lab equipment or tools correctly.
  • Accounting – working with numbers, reports, and financial records.
Key idea
Technical skills are about what you know and can do in a specific field.
Hard skills at work
Knowledge-based and task-specific.
Coding Lab skills Data skills

Soft Skills vs Technical Skills

Both types of skills work together to create a strong professional.

Technical Skills
Job Knowledge

Subject knowledge, tools, and techniques needed to perform specific tasks.

Soft Skills
Behaviour & Attitude

How you communicate, behave, and respond to people and situations.

Important message
Both are important for career success. Employers look for a healthy balance of technical and soft skills.
The winning combination

Soft Skills

Why Employers Value Soft Skills

Employers hire for technical skills but often promote and retain people for soft skills.

  • They need people who can communicate clearly with colleagues and clients.
  • They value employees who can work in teams and support others.
  • They look for individuals who can solve problems and stay calm under pressure.
  • They appreciate responsible behaviour – punctuality, reliability, and honesty.
Beyond the CV
Soft skills show in interviews and daily work.
Responsibility Team spirit Communication

What Is Self Awareness?

Self awareness means understanding yourself – who you are, how you think, and how you affect others.

  • Knowing your strengths and weaknesses.
  • Noticing your thoughts, emotions, and behaviour.
  • Understanding how your actions impact other people.

Understanding different parts of yourself helps you make better choices.

  • Your strengths – what you do well.
  • Your weaknesses – areas that need improvement.
  • Your behaviour – how you act in different situations.
  • Your emotions – how you feel and how you handle feelings.

When you know yourself, you can grow yourself.

  • It helps you improve yourself in a focused way.

Strengths are things you naturally do well or have developed through practice.

Personal Weaknesses

Weaknesses are not permanent failures. They are areas for improvement.

  • Fear of speaking – feeling nervous to share ideas in front of others.
  • Poor time management – difficulty planning or meeting deadlines.
Healthy attitude
Instead of hiding weaknesses, we can notice them and work on them step by step.

How Does Self Improvement Happen?

Change is a process. Small actions over time create big results.

  • Practice – repeating new behaviours until they become habits.
  • Learning – gaining new knowledge from classes, books, or mentors.
  • Feedback – listening to others’ observations and using them to improve.
Remind them: “You don’t need to be perfect. You just need to keep improving.”
Continuous improvement
Learning, trying, and listening.

Positive Mindset

What Is Positive Thinking?

Positive thinking means focusing on solutions and opportunities instead of only on problems.

  • Looking for what you can do instead of what you cannot do.
  • Asking, “What is the next step?” instead of giving up.
  • Believing that challenges can be managed with effort and help.
Choosing your focus
Thoughts shape feelings and actions.
Hope Solutions Possibility
Positive Mindset

Benefits of Positive Thinking

Positive thoughts support better performance and well-being.

  • More confidence – you believe you can learn and improve.
  • Better motivation – you feel more energy to try and keep going.
  • Better problem solving – you think more clearly under pressure.
A helpful mindset
Positive thinking supports action and resilience.
Energy Focus Calm
Positive Mindset

What Is Negative Thinking?

Negative thinking focuses on problems, fear, and what might go wrong.

Example of a negative thought:

Example
“I cannot do this.”

This kind of thought makes it hard to start, practise, or ask for help.

When thoughts hold you back
Awareness is the first step to change them.
Fear Doubt Worry
Positive Mindset

Effects of Negative Thinking

Our thoughts influence our feelings and actions.

  • Low confidence – you stop believing in your abilities.
  • Stress – you feel pressure and tension more easily.
  • Fear of trying – you avoid new experiences or challenges.
Breaking the cycle
Recognising negative thoughts allows you to change them.

Positive Mindset

Fixed Mindset

A fixed mindset is the belief that abilities cannot change.

Common example:

Example thought
“I am not good at this.”

With a fixed mindset, people avoid challenges and give up quickly when work becomes difficult.

Stuck thinking
Believing you cannot change blocks improvement.
Avoidance Fear Giving up
Positive Mindset

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is the belief that skills can improve with effort and the right strategy.

Common example:

Example thought
“I will learn this.”

With a growth mindset, people face challenges, practise more, and learn from mistakes.

Believing in growth
Effort plus time creates new abilities.
Effort Practice Learning
